The Deep Integration Gap

Cloud functionality is fragmenting faster than anyone can integrate it. Every department adopts new SaaS tools. Every tool has an API. And no one is connecting them deeply enough to actually trust the data flowing between them.

You have two bad options today: DIY tools like Zapier and Make that your teams cobble together without IT oversight — creating compliance risks and unreliable data — or enterprise iPaaS platforms like MuleSoft and Workato that demand specialized developers, six-figure contracts, and months to deploy.

Meanwhile, the integrations your business actually needs — the deep, logic-driven connections that make data actionable across departments — simply don't get built.

Shadow IT Integration Debt

Your teams are building Zapier workflows and Make scenarios without IT's knowledge. No error handling. No monitoring. No compliance review. When one breaks at 2 AM and corrupts customer data, you find out from the customer — not from a dashboard.

iPaaS Sticker Shock

Enterprise iPaaS platforms demand specialized developers, extensive training, and contracts that escalate with every connector and every transaction. You're paying enterprise prices for integrations that are still only medium-deep.

The Multi-Department Wall

Marketing needs CRM integrations. Operations needs ERP connections. HR needs HRIS syncs. Finance needs billing reconciliation. Each department fights for engineering resources that never come — or builds fragile workarounds that IT can't support.

What the gap actually looks like

A fitness franchise with 228 locations needs every location's booking data, membership changes, class attendance, and purchase history synced to a single HubSpot instance — with custom objects, bi-directional updates, rate limiting across all locations hitting one API, and per-location segmentation for corporate reporting. A shallow integration creates 228 disconnected data streams. A deep integration understands that when a client cancels next Tuesday's yoga class, their "next scheduled visit" needs to refresh across the entire system — not just in one location's database. That's the difference between data you can act on and data that misleads you.

Workflow architects, not developers

The people building integrations on APIANT don't need to be software engineers. They need domain expertise — understanding how your business actually uses the data, what rules matter, what edge cases to handle. We call them workflow architects. They build visually, leverage the AI Co-Pilot for new API connections, and the platform handles the rest: rate limiting, error handling, retry logic, monitoring, and scaling.

In every enterprise we've spoken with, this person already exists — in IT operations, solutions engineering, or business systems. The technical bar is understanding business logic, not writing code.
